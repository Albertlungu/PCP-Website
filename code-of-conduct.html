Code of Conduct

Code of Conduct for Pre-College Students

As members of the University of Ottawa Pre-College Program, we uphold the highest standards of professionalism, respect, and integrity. The following principles define the expectations and responsibilities for every student in our community.

1. Respect for Others

  • Treat all fellow students, instructors, staff, and guests with courtesy and respect.
  • Listen attentively and supportively during rehearsals, performances, and discussions.
  • Avoid disruptive behavior that interferes with the learning environment or the experience of others.

2. Commitment to Excellence

  • Strive for excellence in musical performance, technique, and interpretation.
  • Attend all rehearsals, coachings, and performances punctually and prepared.
  • Practice regularly and diligently to continually improve individual and ensemble skills.

3. Cooperation and Collaboration

  • Collaborate with fellow students and instructors in a spirit of teamwork and mutual support.
  • Communicate openly and constructively with peers and instructors to resolve conflicts and address concerns.
  • Contribute positively to the success of ensemble rehearsals, coachings, and performances through active participation.

4. Personal Conduct

  • Conduct oneself with honesty, integrity, and professionalism at all times.
  • Respect the property and facilities of the music program and its affiliated institutions.
  • Adhere to all program policies, rules, and regulations, including those regarding safety and security.

5. Representation of the Program

  • Represent the University of Ottawa Pre-College Program with pride and professionalism in all public appearances and interactions.
  • Uphold the reputation and values of the program through positive conduct and exemplary behavior.

6. Diversity and Inclusion

  • Embrace diversity and foster an inclusive, welcoming environment for all members of the music program.
  • Respect and celebrate the unique backgrounds, perspectives, and experiences of fellow students and instructors.

7. Continuous Improvement

  • Seek opportunities for growth and development as musicians, scholars, and individuals.
  • Provide constructive feedback to instructors and program leadership to help enhance the program.

Consequences of Violations

Violations of this code of conduct may result in disciplinary action, including verbal warnings, probation, suspension, or dismissal from the program, depending on the severity and frequency of the offense. No refunds will be issued if a student is dismissed as a result of disciplinary action.

By adhering to this code of conduct, we demonstrate our commitment to a positive, supportive learning environment where all students can thrive and excel.
