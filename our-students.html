Our Students

Nadya Bissett

Nadya Bissett is excited to be playing viola for the first time in any ensemble! Nadya studies violin with Michael van der Sloot and Donnie Deacon, and has recently developed an interest in the viola. She has taken part in the OYOA since she was 11 years old, and is a proud member of the Ottawa Youth Symphony Orchestra. She is going into grade 12 at Canterbury High School where she is a member of the strings program, as well as the Canterbury String Ensemble and Orchestra. Nadya has won a few awards including the Canterbury Instrumental Music- Strings subject excellence award in 2023, First Class Honours with Distinction for RCM grade 8 music theory and Honourable Mention in a Music for Young Children composing competition. Nadya is a lover of all kinds of music, including Old Time/Appalachian fiddle tunes, of which she started a fiddle club for at Canterbury. She also plays the banjo and piano, and is trying her best to learn accordion.

Albert Lungu

Albert Lungu is a grade 10 Merivale pre-IB student with a decade-long passion for the study of violin. He has been a member of the Ottawa Youth Orchestra Academy (OYOA), showcasing his skills both in the Concert (2021-22) and Symphony (2023-24) orchestra. More recently, Albert has expanded his interests for string instruments to include viola, and he is currently studying both violin and viola with Michael Van der Sloot. His love for music has led him to study oboe in high school and perform in the school band.

Albert looks forward to returning in the next school year to the OYOA as first violonist in the Symphony orchestra, and is excited to join the Pre-College Program at the University of Ottawa as a violist. Beyond music, Albert enjoys scuba diving, biking and programming, as well as many other activities and sports.

Kien Nguyen-Vu

Kien Nguyen-Vu, 17 years old, has been learning violin since the age of 4 starting with Ottawa Suzuki Strings until the age of 6. Kien continued his training with Mr Serhii Vyhovskyi for 4 years and was a concertmaster of the JuniorVyhovskyi Strings ensemble. He has been studying with Madam Joan Milkson since 2019 at the Conservatory of Music of Gatineau in the Préparatoire (a pre-collegial level) program in the speciality of violin performance. During the 2021-2022 school year, Kien was the concertmaster of the Préparatoire's Strings Ensemble. He recently completed the Préparatoire program in May 2024.

Kien has been performing at the Young Strings Performers' Foundation and has been a bursary recipient from the Foundation for the past six years. At the Bravissimo Music Competition at the Conservatory, he was one of the 3 finalists in 2020 (the finals were canceled due to the Covid-19 pandemic) and won the First Prize in 2022 in the Préparatoire 12 to 15 year-old category. Kien has also participated in the Charleston International Music Competition and won the First Prize for the 2021 Baroque Music Competition, the Second Prize for the 2021 Romantic Competition, and the First Prize for the 2022 20th Century Music Competition in his group age for violin. During his free time, Kien loves to play volleyball and also trains badminton weekly. He loves to play video games and likes listening to all sorts of music.

Marianne

Marianne has been playing violin for over 12 years and currently studies with Michael van der Sloot and Yosuke Kawasaki. Marianne is currently co-concert master in the Ottawa Youth Symphony Orchestra, has played as concert master and principle second of the Ottawa Pops Orchestra on numerous occasions and has been invited to play as part of the Ottawa Symphony Orchestra. Marianne has competed in several competitions and has won 1st place at the National Capital Region Kiwanis Music Festival level 10 and has been the Second Prize Winner for Best Mozart Performance from Great Composers International Competition. Marianne also enjoys teaching violin and music theory in their free time.

Spencer Song

Spencer Song is a grade 8 student and has been accepted to the IB program at Colonel By High School. He began studying piano at the age of 7 and has developed a strong interest in violin at age of 8. He has distinguished himself locally with numerous scholarships and trophies, including one for the Best Musicianship. He also won awards provincially and nationally at competitions such as CMC. He has consistently received marks of distinction for his RCM exams, winning ORMTA scholarships for both piano and violin, including a national Gold Medal. He also plays the first flute at the All Star Band at school. He has begun composition in Baroque, classical and romantic music. Meanwhile, He started to write orchestral music for percussion, string, brass and wind instruments.

Henrik Stephenson

Henrik Stephenson began playing cello at the age of 6, under the tutelage of Jean-François Marquis. In addition to playing cello, Henrik is also very interested in music composition. He really likes music from the early 20th century and late romantic periods. His favorite composer is Tchaikovsky. Henrik also enjoys hiking and cycling.

Jacob

Jacob has played the cello for 7 years. He has competed 6 times in the Kiwanis Music Festival, where he has been awarded the Richard Casselman Trophy (2019), Pawel Szymczyk Marjanovic trophy (2023 and 2024), and several scholarships, including the Pomer Family Scholarship of $1000. He has also participated in the Ontario Music Festival, achieving Honourable Mention in 2021, 3rd in 2022 and 2nd twice in 2023 and 2024. Aside from solo contests, Jacob has been a member of the Ottawa Youth Symphony Orchestra since 2022, and performed with the Thirteen Strings Chamber Orchestra under the Camerata Junior Strings programme in 2019. In addition, Jacob won first place in the Ontario Music Festival as principal cellist of I Cellisti. As a member of Capital Strings and Voices Collective, he has contributed to the community by playing chamber music at retirement homes, performing at events (Dragon Boat Festival, Migratory Bird Day, Children's Festival, etc.), and teaching children music at the Kitigan Zibi First Nations Reserve. Jacob recently performed with the Cappies Orchestra at NAC in June and has been asked to play at the Rising Stars Chamberfest in July.

Veronika Jovic

My name is Veronika Jovic and I am going to grade nine and will be attending Merivale HS in the fall. I began playing the violin at five years old and am currently studying violin with professor David Thies-Thompson. I have participated in the Vyhovskyi string ensemble and have played with the Strings of St. John's in the performance of Vivaldi's double violin concerto in A Minor. I regularly participate in the Ottawa's Kiwanis Music Festival where I was awarded the George Piers Trophy, Sziladi Family Trophy and Trophée Matthew Froment-Savoie. I am also a recipient of the Lakehead Festival of Music and Arts and Kiwanis Music Festival of London scholarship and trophy awards in a virtual competition. Additionally, I have participated in the provincial Ontario Music Festival Association finals for four years and have placed first in the competition twice. This summer, I will be part of the String Summer School with Donnie Deacon at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland in Glasgow. Other than violin, I enjoy playing volleyball and soccer, learning how to crochet, and drawing cartoons.

Josephine van der Sloot

Josephine van der Sloot began studying the cello at the age of six. Currently, she is a student of Paul Marleyn. Josephine was the Ottawa Youth Concert Orchestra's principal cello until joining the Ottawa Youth Symphony Orchestra this past year. Josie has won numerous awards at the Ottawa Kiwanis Music Festival and was a prize winner at the Provincial Music Festival in 2023. In addition to cello, Josephine is an Honor Roll student at St. Francis Xavier High School and a dancer at the Greta Leeming Studio of Dance. Josie enjoys spending time with her family, reading, and working out in her spare time.

Henry Johnson

Henry Johnson began studying viola at age five. He currently studies privately under Caren Abramoff and has also studied under Donnie Deacon and Paul Casey. Henry is a grade 11 student in the strings music program at Canterbury High School, was principal viola in the Ottawa Youth Orchestra Academy's OJYO (now Ottawa Youth Concert Orchestra), and has won a number of first place awards in the Ottawa Kiwanis Music Festival. In addition to studying viola, Henry enjoys exploring an ever-expanding range of instruments, and plays acoustic, electric and bass guitar, piano and drums. He is also an avid fencer and volleyball player.

Philippe Lafleur

Philippe Lafleur is 15 years old and is going into grade 10 at De La Salle high school this September. He has been taking piano and violin lessons since elementary school and is part of the Ottawa Youth Symphony Orchestra. Philippe won the Edythe Young Browne Memorial Trophy (for senior strings solo) and received the John Gazsi Memorial Scholarship for his violin performances at the 2024 Ottawa Kiwanis Music Festival. Philippe has participated in the uOttawa Summer String Academy, the Music at Port Milford Academy, and the Domaine Forget de Charlevoix International Music and Dance Academy. Philippe also likes to spend time with family and friends, play video games, and read books like The Hunger Games.
