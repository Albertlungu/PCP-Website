Our Students

Henrik Stephenson

Henrik Stephenson began playing cello at the age of 6, under the tutelage of Jean-François Marquis. In addition to playing cello, Henrik is also very interested in music composition. He really likes music from the early 20th century and late romantic periods. His favorite composer is Tchaikovsky. Henrik also enjoys hiking and cycling.

Jacob Kang

Jacob has played the cello for 7 years. He has competed 6 times in the Kiwanis Music Festival, where he has been awarded the Richard Casselman Trophy (2019), Pawel Szymczyk Marjanovic trophy (2023 and 2024), and several scholarships, including the Pomer Family Scholarship of

000. He has also participated in the Ontario Music Festival, achieving Honourable Mention in 2021, 3rd in 2022 and 2nd twice in 2023 and 2024. Aside from solo contests, Jacob has been a member of the Ottawa Youth Symphony Orchestra since 2022, and performed with the Thirteen Strings Chamber Orchestra under the Camerata Junior Strings programme in 2019. In addition, Jacob won first place in the Ontario Music Festival as principal cellist of I Cellisti. As a member of Capital Strings and Voices Collective, he has contributed to the community by playing chamber music at retirement homes, performing at events (Dragon Boat Festival, Migratory Bird Day, Children's Festival, etc.), and teaching children music at the Kitigan Zibi First Nations Reserve. Jacob recently performed with the Cappies Orchestra at NAC in June and has been asked to play at the Rising Stars Chamberfest in July.

Josephine van der Sloot

Josephine van der Sloot began studying the cello at the age of six. Currently, she is a student of Paul Marleyn. Josephine was the Ottawa Youth Concert Orchestra's principal cello until joining the Ottawa Youth Symphony Orchestra this past year. Josie has won numerous awards at the Ottawa Kiwanis Music Festival and was a prize winner at the Provincial Music Festival in 2023. In addition to cello, Josephine is an Honor Roll student at St. Francis Xavier High School and a dancer at the Greta Leeming Studio of Dance. Josie enjoys spending time with her family, reading, and working out in her spare time.

Philippe Lafleur

Philippe Lafleur is 16 years old and is going into grade 11 at De La Salle high school this September. He has been taking piano and violin lessons since elementary school and is part of the Ottawa Youth Symphony Orchestra. Philippe won the Edythe Young Browne Memorial Trophy (for senior strings solo) and received the John Gazsi Memorial Scholarship for his violin performances at the 2024 Ottawa Kiwanis Music Festival. Philippe has participated in the uOttawa Summer String Academy, the Music at Port Milford Academy, and the Domaine Forget de Charlevoix International Music and Dance Academy. Philippe also likes to spend time with family and friends, play video games, and read books like The Hunger Games.
